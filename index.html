Ben Priddy

Ben Priddy is a builder at the seam of creative practice and emerging technology. For the better part of two decades he has moved between studios, startups, and stages — shipping interactive work, leading small teams, and prototyping the kinds of ideas that usually live on whiteboards a little too long. His current obsession is what becomes possible when AI absorbs the grind of software, leaving the interesting decisions — speed, security, beauty — to people.

Today he consults with teams who want their tools to feel as good as they function: GPU-driven interfaces, Rust and WebAssembly where it counts, and AI woven into the practice rather than bolted on. He guides creative organizations through technology decisions, and technology organizations through creative ones — and he builds things like this page to prove the point.
