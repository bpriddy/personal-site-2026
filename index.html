Ben Priddy

I have been writing code, managing small teams, doing creative tech and consulting for 20 years.

Currently I head AI technology globally at the creative agency Anomaly, where I research, educate on, experiment with and build with AI.

I strongly believe that self-attention and transformer architecture have so wildly over-solved the NLP problem that the momentum carried us straight through to other cognitive functions that overlap the language space, like reasoning and world knowledge. We continue to push transformer-based models as far as they will go, supported by scaling laws and incredibly smart technology for incremental gains, but I think this doesn't bring us to the true vision of AGI.

I believe a handful of foundational algorithms are still ahead of us to innovate. Reason, though possible in the language space, is theoretically far simpler to represent in another architecture. Once transformers' responsibility is reduced to an interface layer, language, and routing in the latent space to other more optimized architectures for other cognitive functions, transformers will necessarily shrink by orders of magnitude. The dependence on compute will reduce. The scale of training data may even reduce.

Even with how staggeringly knowledgeable and skillful the SOTA AI is right now, I think it's likely dwarfed by the hybrid systems of the near future.
